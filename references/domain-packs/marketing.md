## Grill Lenses for Marketing

| Lens | Perspective | Focus |
|------|------------|-------|
| **Customer / Audience** | Human reality | Does the message match what the target audience actually believes, fears, or wants? What objection is left unanswered? Is the positioning believable to someone who has never heard of this product? What would make them scroll past? |
| **Skeptic** | Alternative view | What makes this unbelievable, forgettable, or indistinguishable from competitors? What does a competitor do better? What claim can't be substantiated? What would a critical journalist write? |
| **Brand / Legal** | System view | Does the message fit within the brand voice and positioning? Are all claims accurate and legally defensible? What regulatory constraints apply (advertising standards, data privacy disclosures, sector-specific rules)? |

## Definition of Done — Marketing

Marketing work is done when **all** of the following are true:

- [ ] Message validated with a representative sample of the target audience (user research, interviews, survey, or A/B test — not just internal review)
- [ ] Key metrics defined and measurable before launch (not "we'll measure success after")
- [ ] All claims are substantiated with evidence (data, citations, verified product capabilities)
- [ ] Legal / compliance review completed (advertising claims, data disclosures, sector regulations)
- [ ] Brand consistency verified against voice, tone, and positioning guidelines
- [ ] Assets reviewed for accuracy, grammar, and visual consistency
- [ ] Distribution channels confirmed and assets formatted correctly per channel
- [ ] Success criteria documented and agreed upon before launch

## What to Verify

### Message effectiveness
- Does the headline communicate the primary value proposition in under 5 seconds?
- Does the message address the target audience's primary concern or objection?
- Is the call-to-action clear and does it ask for exactly one thing?

### Claim accuracy
- Every factual claim has a source that can be cited if challenged
- Comparative claims (e.g., "faster than X", "best in class") are verified and defensible
- No unsupported superlatives or vague claims ("world-class", "revolutionary", "leading")

### Legal and compliance
- Applicable advertising standards reviewed (ASA, FTC, or equivalent per jurisdiction)
- Required disclosures present (privacy, financial, medical, or other regulated categories)
- Trademark and copyright checked for all third-party references and assets

### Channel-specific verification
- Format and dimensions correct per platform (social, email, print, digital)
- Copy length within platform limits
- Visual assets render correctly across devices and dark/light modes where applicable

## Marketing Principles

- **Test the message, not just the design.** Visual polish does not fix a message that doesn't land with the audience.
- **Define metrics before launch.** Measuring success after the fact produces rationalization, not learning.
- **Unsubstantiated claims are a liability.** If a claim cannot be defended with evidence, remove it.
- **The audience's mental model matters more than the internal one.** How the team describes the product and how the audience understands it are often different.
- **Assets are a dependency graph too.** A headline change cascades to subheadings, CTAs, and social copy. Plan the ownership of shared messaging the same way as shared code.
